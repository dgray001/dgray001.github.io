CUF Membership

Catholics United for the Faith is called an apostolate because membership is ordered to apostolic action. Members first seek personal holiness, so that, should it be God’s will, they are ready instruments for apostolic action.

As a lay apostolate, Catholics United for the Faith provides a cooperative structure through which the laity can be effective in their vocation. While an individual might have limited resources, the combined resources of thousands united in effort bring about a greater effect. When an individual seeks to express himself, he remains but one voice, whereas combined with others in an apostolate, his voice is amplified. In turn, the individual benefits from association, through resources and support, in living out his personal vocation.

Whether a person is consecrated, married, single, or ordained; members of an ecclesial movement, religious order, or secular; adherents of a particular piety, spirituality, or devotion; CUF advances the general call to holiness and vocation of the laity and supports each individual’s personal call, however that is manifested.

Benefits of CUF Membership

  • Spiritual support and assistance
    • Masses offered regularly for members
    • Intentions remembered by CUF staff daily with the Angelus & weekly with Mass & Adoration
    • United in prayer with other members throughout the world
  • Lay Witness newsletter connects you with like-minded members & initiatives
  • Chapter Membership and Development
    • Collaborate with like-minded Catholics in apostolic activities, prayer, & fellowship
    • Assistance to charter & develop a Chapter if one does not exist in your local area
    • Formation materials for Chapters
  • Personal Formation Assistance
    • Unrestricted access to Information Services in digital and hard copy
    • Over 270 FAITH FACTS
    • In-depth research tailored to your unique questions about the Catholic Faith
    • Guidance from expert staff
    • Access to the CUF research library
  • Speakers Bureau to help you make local events formative and successful

Benefits of CUF Membership

  • Accept the CUF Mission to “unite the faithful from all walks of life in order to support, defend, and advance the efforts of the teaching Church” as your own
  • Support CUF spiritually by praying for the apostolate and its members daily
  • Support CUF morally by using & sharing its resources & encouraging participation in the apostolate
  • Engage in apostolic activity in keeping with your state in life
  • When possible, support CUF financially

For a more in-depth treatment of the obligations and benefits of CUF membership, please see Lay Witness 36.4.

While most CUF supporters choose to become members, some choose to become CUF Associates. CUF Associates support CUF’s work, usually through prayer and donation, without being considered members and without taking on the obligations of membership. CUF Associates still receive Lay Witness.

To become a CUF Member or Associate, please contact us.

CUF Chapters

CUF members residing in the same area often join together so that their efforts in support of the Catholic faith may bear more fruit. Whenever such a group fulfills the established requirements, it may be given official recognition and charter as a CUF chapter. Chapter members work together to promote authentic spiritual renewal in their own personal lives, in the lives of their families, and in the life of the wider community which comes under their influence. The chapter seeks ways to help its members become knowledgeable about the doctrine and discipline of the Church. The chapter also assists and directs the efforts of its individual members in supporting the efforts of the international office of CUF.

The chapter thus provides a local organized structure for supporting the work of the apostolate as a whole. In times of confusion and uncertainty the CUF chapter and all CUF members strive to be a source of stability by bearing clear and unequivocal witness to the authentic doctrine and discipline of the Church in strict fidelity to the Pope and the bishops in union with him.

The international office of CUF is located in Hopedale, Ohio, USA. There is a CUF national office in Wellington, New Zealand, as well as individual members all over the world.

If you are interested in starting a CUF Chapter, please contact us.

Charter a New Chapter

We must apply CUF’s mission to address specific, local challenges to faith and orthodoxy. Our chapter members and officers are committed to doing the work of the Church in allegiance to her venerable traditions and her holy teaching authority.

CUF members understand that they cannot proceed effectively without exemplifying in themselves the primacy of the spiritual-sacramental order of which the Church is the guardian. Our chapter members recognize that they can do much to determine whether a true renewal will occur in the Church or whether forces openly hostile to Catholic doctrine, under the guise of a false “renewal,” will win the day.

A great need of our day for Catholics is a renewed sense of the adventure of the Church as a guardian of doctrine. She is the champion of the fixed and eternal, and she will always instruct the proud world which finds her out of fashion and mocks her for her steadfastness.

– Adapted from A Personal Message to Future CUF Chapter Officers from CUF’s founding President, H. Lyman Stebbins

If you are interested in chartering a new chapter of Catholics United for the Faith, please contact us to request a Chapter Formation Manual. This brief document outlines why we are convinced CUF chapters are so needed for the renewal of the Church, the kind of apostolic work CUF chapters do, the spirit in which this work is carried out, and the concrete steps you would need to take to charter a chapter. Requesting a manual places you under no obligation to charter a chapter.

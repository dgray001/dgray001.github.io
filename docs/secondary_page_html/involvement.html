The Catholic Church identifies those of us living the Faith in the World as “The Church Militant”. Those in Purgatory are “The Church Suffering” and those in Heaven are “The Church Triumphant”. To secure our place in Heaven, we must not accept the lies of the World. The ruler of the World is the Father of Lies and the author of the Culture of Death. As dedicated Catholics, we must oppose the lies, the darkness and the death.

From the early days of Christianity, Christians have called themselves “Soldiers for Christ” to emphasize the constant struggle we endure this side of Heaven. Our baptismal promises include a renunciation of the powers of sin and darkness. We renew that promise during every Easter Triduum. At our baptism, we were given the power to convert the world from within; to be in the world but not of the world (c.f. John 15:18-19).

Catholics United for the Faith expects all her members to engage in spiritual and moral warfare with an intention to advance the Gospel of Jesus Christ and His Church. That is the Gospel of Life. That is revolutionary.

What Can You Do?

Members are CUF’s cause to exist and its greatest resource. Our Mission is “to unite the faithful from all walks of life so they might support, defend, and advance the Teaching Office of the Church.” We cannot succeed in a defense of the Gospel of Life if we do not unite in the Faith. CUF’s International Office is purposed to encourage membership, unite members in local chapters, and provide resources that provide moral and spiritual formation in keeping with the Word of God. And, we provide resources to assist members and associates in their individual pursuits of apostolic activities in keeping with our baptismal promises.

We are here for you, but you are here for Christ and His Church; to be the Salt of the Earth and convert the world from within. We want you to succeed.

How You Can Help

First, pray for the success of CUF. If God does not build they house, they labor in vain who build it (Psalm 127:1). Pray that we, and all CUF members, conform our efforts to the Will of God.

Second, encourage engagement with us. I am happy to visit a Chapter, or even just a group of members who are not formed as a Chapter. I have given talks on a variety of topics, and I would be happy to come visit and encourage you in your Faith. Another means of engagement would be to call Information Services and use our Faith Facts in personal formation and apostolic outreach. Our materials make a great starting point to engage family, friends, or visitors on matters of the Faith. If you are not sure what we have, call and ask. We will gladly send a list of our Faith Facts.

Finally, I urge you to seek the Kingdom of God within you. Men of violence are taking the Kingdom of God by force (c.f. Mt. 11:12), one soul at a time. Don’t be a casualty. Join me in this Golden Anniversary of Catholics United for the Faith, as we gather the sheep into the fold of the One True Shepherd, Jesus Christ."

-Philip C. L. Gray, President (Lay Witness Vol. 36, No. 1)

CUF Membership

Catholics United for the Faith is called an apostolate because membership is ordered to apostolic action. Members first seek personal holiness, so that they are ready instruments for evangelistic action.

As a lay apostolate, Catholics United for the Faith provides a cooperative structure through which the laity can be effective in their vocation. While an individual might have limited resources, the combined resources of thousands united in effort bring about a greater effect. When an individual seeks to express himself, he remains but one voice. Whereas, combined with others in an apostolate, his voice is amplified. In turn, the individual benefits from association, through resources and support, in living out his personal vocation.

Whether a person is consecrated, married, single, or ordained; members of an ecclesial movement, religious order, or secular; adherents of a particular piety, spirituality, or devotion; CUF advances the general call to holiness and vocation of the laity and supports each individual’s personal call, however that is manifested.

Benefits of CUF Membership

  • Spiritual support and assistance
    • Masses offered regularly for members
    • Intentions remembered by CUF staff daily with the Angelus & weekly with Mass & Adoration
    • United in prayer with other members throughout the world
  • Lay Witness newsletter connects you with like-minded members & initiatives
  • Chapter Membership and Development
    • Collaborate with like-minded Catholics in apostolic activities, prayer, & fellowship
    • Assistance to charter & develop a Chapter if one does not exist in your local area
    • Formation materials for Chapters
  • Personal Formation Assistance
    • Unrestricted access to Information Services in digital and hard copy
    • Over 270 Faith Facts
    • In-depth research tailored to your unique questions about the Catholic Faith
    • Guidance from expert staff
    • Access to the CUF research library
  • Speakers Bureau to help you make local events formative and successful

Obligations of CUF Membership

  • Accept the CUF Mission to “unite the faithful from all walks of life in order to support, defend, and advance the efforts of the teaching Church” as your own
  • Support CUF spiritually by praying for the apostolate and its members daily
  • Support CUF morally by using & sharing its resources & encouraging participation in the apostolate
  • Engage in apostolic activity in keeping with your state in life
  • When possible, support CUF financially

For a more in-depth treatment of the obligations and benefits of CUF membership, please see Lay Witness 36.4.

While most CUF supporters choose to become members, some choose to become CUF Associates. CUF Associates support CUF’s work, usually through prayer and donation, without being considered members and without taking on the obligations of membership. CUF Associates still receive Lay Witness.

To become a CUF Member or Associate, please contact us.

CUF Chapters

Catholics United for the Faith encourages members to unite in a common endeavor. CUF members residing in the same area often join together so that their efforts in support of the Catholic faith may bear more fruit. Whenever such a group fulfills the established requirements, it may be given official recognition and charter as a CUF chapter. Chapter members work together to promote authentic spiritual and moral renewal in their own personal lives, in the lives of their families, and in the life of the wider community which comes under their influence. The chapter seeks ways to help its members become knowledgeable about the doctrines and disciplines of the Church. The chapter also assists and directs the efforts of its individual members in supporting the efforts of the international office of CUF.

The chapter provides a local, organized structure for supporting the work of the apostolate as a whole. In times of confusion and uncertainty the CUF chapter and all CUF members strive to be a source of stability by bearing clear witness to the authentic doctrines and disciplines of the Church in fidelity to the Pope and the bishops in union with him.

The international office of CUF is located in Hopedale, Ohio, USA.

If you are interested in starting a CUF Chapter, please contact us.
